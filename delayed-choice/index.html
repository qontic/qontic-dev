At the beam splitter

The incoming wave packet encounters a balanced splitter. Its wave passes into both arms, while each Bohmian particle has one continuous trajectory guided by the local wave field. Change the momentum to see how the spatial and temporal scales of the motion respond.

Wave splitting and individual trajectories

The two trajectories are samples from the same wave-guided flow, not two fragments of one particle.

A trajectory ensemble

Many initial positions sampled from \( |\psi|^2 \) reveal the statistical structure of the motion. The detector histogram records where trajectories arrive. Adjust the particle count to compare a sparse sample with a smoother empirical distribution.

Particle ensemble and detection histogram

As the sample grows, the detected ensemble more clearly represents the quantum probability flow.

Make the delayed choice

Start the simulation and use the in-scene Detector button while the packet is moving. With the detector off, registration is deferred to the far wall. Switching it on restores the nearer detector. The later choice changes the subsequent experimental arrangement, but it does not rewrite the particle's earlier trajectory.

Interactive detector choice

Click Detector OFF or Detector ON during the run to change where detection occurs.

Theory & Equations

Wave evolution

The wave function evolves under the time-dependent Schrodinger equation. The simulated splitter, guide mirrors, and detector arrangement are represented through the potential \(V(\mathbf{x},t)\).

Schrodinger equation \[ i\hbar\frac{\partial\psi}{\partial t} = \left(-\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\nabla^2+V(\mathbf{x},t)\right)\psi \]

Balanced splitting

An ideal balanced splitter produces two branches with equal squared amplitudes. The relative phase between the branches remains part of the full wave function.

Two-arm state \[ \psi \longrightarrow \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left(\psi_{\mathrm{upper}}+e^{i\phi}\psi_{\mathrm{lower}}\right) \]

Bohmian guidance

Writing \(\psi=R e^{iS/\hbar}\), the probability current and velocity field are \(\mathbf{j}=\rho\nabla S/m\) and \(\mathbf{v}=\mathbf{j}/\rho\). The actual particle position follows one integral curve of this field.

Guidance equation \[ \frac{d\mathbf{X}}{dt} = \left.\frac{\mathbf{j}}{|\psi|^2}\right|_{\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{X}(t)} = \left.\frac{\nabla S}{m}\right|_{\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{X}(t)} \]

Detection statistics

If initial positions are distributed according to the Born density, the continuity equation preserves that distribution during the motion. Detector counts therefore approach the flux predicted by the evolving wave.

Equivariance \[ \rho_B(\mathbf{x},0)=|\psi(\mathbf{x},0)|^2 \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \rho_B(\mathbf{x},t)=|\psi(\mathbf{x},t)|^2 \]

The delayed choice

The detector setting may be changed after launch. In the simulation this changes \(V(\mathbf{x},t)\) and the later boundary conditions. The wave and guidance field then evolve from their current state under the new arrangement. No backward-in-time update of the earlier particle position is required.
