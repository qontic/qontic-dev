Theory & Equations

1 — Hydrogen Eigenstates

The time-independent Schrödinger equation Hψ = Eψ for hydrogen has exact solutions labelled by three integers:

ψn,l,m(r,θ,φ) = Rnl(r) · Ylm(θ,φ)
Symbol Name Range Controls
n Principal 1, 2, 3… Energy: En = −13.6/n² eV
l Angular 0 … n−1 Shape: s(sphere), p(dumbbell), d(clover)…
m Magnetic −l … +l Orientation & circulation speed

Rnl are the associated Laguerre radial functions; Ylm are spherical harmonics. The orbital density is ρ = |ψ|².

2 — Bohmian (de Broglie–Bohm) Mechanics

Particles have definite positions at all times, guided by a velocity field derived from the wave function:

v = (ħ/me) Im(∇ψ / ψ)

In atomic units (ħ = me = 1) this simplifies to v = Im(∇ψ/ψ).
For a real wave function Im(∇ψ/ψ) = 0, so particles are completely static — this is why m=0 orbitals show frozen particles without spin.
For m ≠ 0: ψ ∝ eimφ, giving azimuthal circulation at speed vφ = m / (r sinθ).
The key theorem (equivariance) guarantees that if particles start distributed as |ψ|², they remain so forever — the distribution is preserved by the flow.

3 — Pauli–Bohm Spin Extension

For a spin-½ particle the wave function becomes a two-component spinor:

Ψ = (ψ↑, ψ↓)T    ρ = |ψ↑|² + |ψ↓|²

The guidance equation gains a second term, the spin-curl:

vP = Im(Ψ†∇Ψ) / ρ  +  (∇×S) / (2ρ)

where S = Ψ†σΨ is the spin density vector and σ are the Pauli matrices.
The first term is the standard convective current (zero for real or m=0 orbitals).
The second term is new: it is non-zero whenever the spin texture S(r) has a curl — i.e. whenever the spin density varies in space with a vortex structure.

Consequence: a 2p0 orbital (m=0, frozen in scalar Bohm) acquires moving particles when spin is added, because ∇×S = ∇ρ × n̂ ≠ 0 whenever ∇ρ and n̂ are not everywhere parallel — which holds for any choice of spin direction, including n̂ = 𝒙. This is the core new physics from the Bohm–Schiller–Tiomno framework.

Analytical prediction — 1s + product spin

For 1s ⊗ |n̂⟩ (orbital × fixed spin direction):
S = ρ(r) n̂  ⇒  ∇×S = ∇ρ × n̂
For 1s: ∇ρ = −2ρ r̂,  so:

v = −(r̂ × n̂)    |v| = sin ∠(r̂, n̂)

The speed is independent of r — particles orbit the spin axis at a constant speed set only by their angular position. This is analytically verifiable (see Expert → Verify below).

4 — Spin Modes in this App

Product mode

Ψ = ψn,l,m(r) ⊗ |n̂⟩   where   |n̂⟩ = cos(θ/2)|↑⟩ + eiφsin(θ/2)|↓⟩
The θ slider rotates the spin axis; φ rotates it azimuthally. This is a coherent spin state on the Bloch sphere. Orbital and spin degrees of freedom are independent (not entangled).
Physically this approximates the state immediately after optical pumping, before spin-orbit coupling entangles the two sectors.

j-state mode

The full hydrogen Hamiltonian includes the spin-orbit term HSO ∝ L·S. Its eigenstates are labelled by total angular momentum j = l ± ½ and projection mj. They are built from orbital and spin components via Clebsch–Gordan coefficients:

|j=3/2, mj=+1/2⟩ = √(2/3)|m=0⟩|↑⟩ + √(1/3)|m=+1⟩|↓⟩

These are the physically correct stationary states of hydrogen including fine structure. The j-state dropdown auto-builds all valid states for whatever (n,l) orbital is currently selected.

Note: for l=0 (s orbitals), L = 0 so spin–orbit coupling vanishes. The j-states reduce to pure |↑⟩ (mj=+½) or |↓⟩ (mj=−½), equivalent to product mode with θ=0 or θ=π. For any other spin direction, use product mode.

5 — Approximations & Limitations

  • Non-relativistic: the Dirac equation is not used. Fine-structure energies are not simulated, only the spatial guidance.
  • No radiation reaction: the electron does not emit photons along its Bohmian trajectory.
  • No electron–electron interaction: hydrogen only (one electron).
  • Product states are not physical eigenstates when l ≠ 0 — they are initial conditions that would evolve under HSO.
  • The spin-curl velocity is computed by finite differences (h = 0.07 a₀). Numerical errors are largest near nodal surfaces where ρ → 0.
