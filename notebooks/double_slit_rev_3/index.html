5. A finite well has bound states and above-barrier scattering

For E<V₀, the wavefunction is oscillatory inside and exponentially decaying outside. For E>V₀, a wave incident from the left is partly reflected and transmitted through the finite well.

The bound-state model solves even and odd matching conditions and normalizes over all x. The scattering model solves continuity of Ψ and dΨ/dx at both well edges and reports the flux coefficients R and T.

7. Orthodox and Bohmian accounts use the same calculated wavefunction

The interpretation changes the account of measurement and configuration, not the Schrödinger evolution or energy spectrum. The default superposition has nonzero current, so the Bohmian view begins with visible guidance motion.

Gold dots in the Bohmian view are independent configurations sampled from |Ψ(x,t)|². Larger gold markers follow dX/dt=j/|Ψ|². They represent distinct identically prepared one-particle systems, not multiple particles in one well.

Interpretation note
Gold dots are independent configurations sampled from the displayed |Ψ(x,t)|² distribution.

Orthodox interpretation

Ψ is a probability amplitude. The Born rule states that |Ψ|² gives the density of possible position-measurement outcomes. No additional particle position is assigned between measurements by this interpretation.

Bohmian mechanics

Ψ guides a definite configuration through v=j/|Ψ|². Gold dots represent separate possible positions across an ensemble of identically prepared one-particle systems. In a single real energy eigenstate, j=0 and every configuration is stationary; a superposition gives nonzero guidance flow.
