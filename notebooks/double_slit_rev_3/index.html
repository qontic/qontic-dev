3. de Broglie wavelength and photon energy

Every quantum object obeys the de Broglie relation λ=h/p. For light, p=Eγ/c, so the same relation becomes Eγ=hc/λ. Shorter wavelengths therefore carry larger photon energies.

This optical notebook uses the photon form of the de Broglie relation. A particle-mass parameter is not used because photons are massless.

Curve: photon energy versus optical de Broglie wavelength. Green marker: selected laboratory wavelength.

6. Which-path information reduces interference

Coherence is the survival of a stable relative phase between the fields from the two slits. When that phase is randomized by environmental coupling, source bandwidth, path marking, or averaging, the interference cross term is reduced.

The coherence factor γ rescales only the interference cross term: γ=1 is fully coherent and γ=0 is the incoherent sum of two single-slit intensities. It does not change the wavelength, slit geometry, or fringe locations. This graph uses the same fixed ±20 mm detector span as Part 5, so changing λ or L visibly changes the pattern while changing γ reduces contrast.

7. Orthodox and Bohmian accounts use the same calculated pattern

The calculated screen probability distribution is unchanged. The interpretations differ in whether they treat an unmeasured particle as having a definite position and path.

Interpretation note

Orthodox interpretation

Ψ is a probability amplitude. The Born rule assigns the screen density |Ψ|². A detection is localized, but this interpretation does not assign a definite unobserved path from source to screen.

Bohmian mechanics

Bohmian mechanics supplements the wavefunction with definite particle configurations guided by the wave. For this optical scalar-field model, the gold curves use the calculated scalar current as an optical guidance analogue. The incoming source-to-slit segments are schematic preparation paths; the post-slit curves are forward current-transport paths derived from the longitudinal scalar current. This is not a complete relativistic Bohmian photon theory.
