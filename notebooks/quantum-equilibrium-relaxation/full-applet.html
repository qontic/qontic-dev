Quantum Equilibrium Relaxation

Theory

A particle in a hard rectangular box has standing-wave energy eigenstates. Superpositions of several eigenstates make the density breathe, shear, and form moving nodal lines.

ψ(x,y,t) = ∑k ck sin(nx,kπx/Lx) sin(ny,kπy/Ly) e-iEkt/ℏ

Bohmian particles are advanced by the probability current. They do not push the wave around; the wave guides them. The Pauli option adds a spin-current term, using the same 2D form as the tunneling demo.

ṙ = jρ,    j = ℏm Im(ψ*∇ψ),    &rho = |\psi|2
ṙPauli = jρ + ℏsmρ (∂yρ, -∂xρ)

Use a non-Born initial particle distribution to watch the particle cloud stretch and fold under a time-dependent wave. Born-rule seeding starts in quantum equilibrium.

The ground state is included as a stationary reference. The superposition presets mix x and y quantum numbers so the trajectories are genuinely two-dimensional.
