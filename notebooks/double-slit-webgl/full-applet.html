Bohmian Double Slit

Theory

A quantum wave packet travels according to the Schrodinger equation. The barrier splits it into two coherent parts; after the slits those parts overlap and form interference.

iℏ∂ψ∂t = [ -ℏ22m∇2 + V ]ψ

The brightness is the Born density. The phase view colors the argument of the same wave.

ρ(x,t) = |ψ|2,    ψ = ReiS/ℏ

A Bohmian particle has a definite position, and its velocity is determined by the probability current. The guiding law is:

ẋ = ∇Sm = jρ,    j = ℏm Im(ψ*∇ψ)

The Pauli option adds an extra term in the current arising from the spin. Paths can change while the same wave density still governs the pattern.

ẋ = jρ + ℏszmρ (∂yρ, -∂xρ)
